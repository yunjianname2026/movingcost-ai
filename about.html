✦ Our Story

Built by someone who
has actually moved.

MovingCOST.ai was born from lived experience — not a whiteboard. We know what it feels like to start over somewhere new, and we built the tool we wish we'd had.

Why We Built This

Moving is never just a logistics problem.

"It is a life decision — one that touches your finances, your family, your identity, and your sense of home."

Our founder is an artist, collector, and entrepreneur who has navigated multiple major relocations across continents and cultures over the course of his life.

Each move carried its own weight: new cities, new systems, new costs, new uncertainties. And each time, the process of figuring it all out felt far harder than it should have been.

Most people are forced to make these decisions with scattered information, contradictory estimates, and no real clarity on what things will actually cost on the other side.

That gap is what MovingCOST.ai was built to close. We combine AI-powered cost planning, city comparison, and practical relocation guidance to help individuals and families move with greater clarity, confidence, and control — whether you're crossing a border for the first time or planning your fifth chapter in a new place.

Our Mission

Move smarter.
Spend less. Live better.

We believe the decision to move your life somewhere new deserves better tools. Not generic averages. Not outdated forum posts. Real intelligence, built around your actual situation.

How It Works

Good decisions come from good information.

Not generic city averages. Not outdated blog posts. Not rough guesses from strangers online.

Every plan we generate is built around your life — your income, your lifestyle, your priorities, and your timeline. Our AI draws on real cost data across 180+ cities to give you a picture that's honest, specific, and actually useful.

We also know the limits of AI. Every report includes clear disclaimers, cost ranges rather than false precision, and specific recommendations on when to engage a professional — because some decisions are too important to get wrong.
